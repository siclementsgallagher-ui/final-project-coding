The main design concet is to create a house which has a close relationship with nature and this archieved by having a swimming-pool linking the house with the landscape and ultimately visual connections with the sea. The idea of connection is reinforced by having the media-room with a window that allows diffuse natural light in and allows views out into the pool.

The curved roofs, which symbolizing the seas waves, also empathize the idea of the nearby sea. These are almost totally covered with a thin bendable photovoltaic panals supplying enough energy to the house, while the remaining area is used as a green roof giving residents some outdoor leisure spaces.

Because of the prominent raised nature of the site and the prominent placing of the building overlooking the road we decided to make the building appear light amd to 'float' with large cantilevers of thin elements to reduce the apparent mass of the building seen from the road. The site sits on the busy Cluny Road facing the enterence to the botanic gardens. From the road the sites sloped up steeply to a platform level many metres above road level.

Like mentioned, before, there is an outdoor garden after walking out from the kitchen. To make the most of the surrounding greenery, landscaped ponds and courtyards were created as focal loci, both the extend visual links to this barrowed landscape, and so serparate the varios wings of the house for the purpose of maintaining privacy.
